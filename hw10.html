Comments and Conclusions

The survey shows that online shopping is common among the people interviewed. Most participants have already purchased online, and the largest group buys online about once a month. Younger participants were more comfortable with online shopping, but older participants also reported using e-commerce when the process was simple and trustworthy.

The results match wider e-commerce trends. Pew Research Center reported that online shopping had become a normal part of buying behavior in the United States, and the U.S. Census Bureau reported that e-commerce sales accounted for 16.4 percent of total U.S. retail sales in 2025.

My conclusion is that e-commerce is popular because it saves time, allows easy price comparison, and offers many product choices. Trust, delivery speed, and return policies are still important factors that influence whether people complete a purchase.
